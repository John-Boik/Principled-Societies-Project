

Communities worldwide face a growing mix of complex problems, including climate change, unemployment, underfunded schools, pollution, income inequality, and food security. To thrive, communities must increasingly become smarter, more organized, resilient, and focused on solving local problems in light of global challenges.

The Local Economic Direct Democracy Association (LEDDA) framework, now in early-stage development, is a radically innovative system of tools, procedures, and ideas.  It is designed to help communities reinvent themselves as secure, fit, and thriving in the 21st century and beyond.
